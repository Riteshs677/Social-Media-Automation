Linda Yaccarino just announced she's stepping down as CEO of X, with no stated reason and no successor named, as she expressed gratitude to X's owner Elon Musk.

Yaccarino's departure comes the same day the company is set to launch its Grok 4 AI model, while Grok 3 has repeatedly made antisemitic remarks in response to prompts about a wide range of topics. It’s only the latest controversy in a tenure marked by Twitter’s rebrand to X, its AI pivot, “everything app” ambitions, and a string of backlashes that opened the door for rivals like Threads and Bluesky to court disillusioned users.
